The <ins class="diffchange diffchange-inline">word</ins> "<ins class="diffchange diffchange-inline">coronavirus</ins>" was first used in print in 1968 by an informal group of virologists in the journal ''[[Nature (journal)|Nature]]'' to designate the new family of viruses.&lt;ref name=":2" /&gt; <ins class="diffchange diffchange-inline">It was immediately adopted by the International Committee for the Nomenclature of Viruses (later renamed [[International Committee on Taxonomy of Viruses]]).&lt;ref&gt;{{Cite journal|last=Lalchhandama|first=Kholhring|date=2020|title=</ins>The <ins class="diffchange diffchange-inline">chronicles of coronaviruses: the bronchitis, the hepatitis and the common cold|url=https://www.sciencevision.org/issue/44/article/293|journal=Science Vision|language=en|volume=20|issue=1|pages=43–53|doi=10.33493/scivis.20.01.04|via=}}&lt;/ref&gt;
