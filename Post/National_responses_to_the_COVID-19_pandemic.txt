The first case of COVID-19 in Scotland was confirmed on March 1st with the first death of someone with COVID-19 occurring 12 days later. The following week, on March 20th, the Scottish government instructed a number of businesses to close, including pubs, cafes, cinemas and bingo halls. On 21 May, First Minister [[Nicola Sturgeon]] outlined a four-phase "route map" for easing lockdown restrictions in Scotland to take effect from 28 May subject to the number of new cases of COVID-19 continuing to fall.
